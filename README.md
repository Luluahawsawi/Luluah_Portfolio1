### 1. Hajj Conference & Exhibition Dashboard
**Tool:** Power BI (DAX, Power Query)
**Data Source:** Published Hajj Conference & Exhibition official reports

**Objective:**
To track and visualize the growth and development of the Hajj Conference across multiple years, measuring how key indicators evolved from one edition to the next.

**Key Questions Answered:**
- How has visitor attendance grown across conference editions?
- What is the trend in participating countries and social reach over time?
- How have workshops and exhibition area scaled year on year?

**Key Insights:**
- Total visitors reached 315K across all editions, with consistent growth each year
- Social reach hit 348M, reflecting increasing global interest in the event
- Participating countries grew to 305, demonstrating the conference's expanding international footprint
- Workshop attendance surged from 0.5K in 2022 to 10K in 2025 — a 20x increase

**Skills Demonstrated:**
DAX measures · Time intelligence · KPI cards · Bar and column charts · Multi-year trend analysis · Bilingual dashboard design

### 2. Walmart Promotion Analysis Dashboard
**Tool:** Power BI
**Data Source:** Walmart retail transactions dataset (YouTube tutorial project, self-executed)

**Objective:**
To analyze whether promotional activity meaningfully impacts revenue outcomes, and which promotion types and store locations drive the strongest results.

**Key Questions Answered:**
- Does running promotions lead to higher revenue compared to no promotion?
- Which promotion type — BOGO, Percentage Discount, or None — performs best?
- How does promotional revenue vary across store locations over time?

**Key Insights:**
- Revenue with promotion (8.06M) outperformed revenue without promotion (7.20M) — a 12% uplift
- Percentage Discount drove the largest share of promotional revenue
- Chicago and New York locations showed the strongest promotional response
- Revenue peaks were visible mid-year, suggesting seasonal sensitivity to promotions

**Skills Demonstrated:**
Slicers and filters · Date range analysis · KPI cards · Stacked bar charts · Multi-location trend lines · Revenue comparison

### 3. Marketing Budget Waterfall Analysis
**Tool:** Power BI
**Data Source:** Hypothetical marketing budget scenario (self-built)

**Objective:**
To demonstrate how a waterfall chart can clearly communicate to leadership where a marketing budget goes — breaking down gross revenue into net return by surfacing each cost category as a visible deduction.

**Key Questions Answered:**
- Where exactly does the marketing budget get consumed between gross revenue and net return?
- Which cost categories have the biggest impact on net outcome?
- How can marketers present budget accountability to non-technical managers?

**Key Insights:**
- Agency & tools fees and unqualified lead loss represented the largest deductions from gross revenue
- The waterfall format makes budget erosion immediately visible — something a pie chart or table cannot achieve
- Net return settled at approximately 0.3M against a 0.5M gross revenue starting point
- The visualization is designed to support real marketing budget review conversations with senior stakeholders

**Skills Demonstrated:**
Waterfall chart design · DAX calculations · Budget variance storytelling · Arabic-language dashboard · Executive-ready visualization

### 4. HR Analytics Dashboard
**Tool:** Power BI
**Data Source:** HR dataset provided by course instructor (tutorial project, self-executed)

**Objective:**
To investigate whether employee attrition follows predictable patterns, and surface actionable insights that help organizations make informed retention decisions.

**Key Questions Answered:**
- Do employees leave for identifiable, predictable reasons?
- Which departments, age groups, and salary bands carry the highest attrition risk?
- How can leadership use data to reduce attrition proactively?

**Key Insights:**
- Overall attrition rate stands at 16% across 1,480 employees — with Research Scientists accounting for the highest count at 100 departures
- Employees aged 18–25 show the highest attrition rate at 36%, dropping significantly with age — suggesting early tenure is the most critical retention window
- Employees earning up to 5K show 22% attrition — more than five times the rate of those earning 15K+, pointing to salary as a primary driver
- Attrition peaks sharply at year 1 of tenure (59 employees) then stabilizes — indicating onboarding and early experience are decisive factors

**Skills Demonstrated:**
Multi-tab dashboard design · Donut and bar charts · Trend line analysis · Attrition rate calculations · Department and role-level segmentation · HR domain analytics
